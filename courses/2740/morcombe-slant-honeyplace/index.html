Common Law

Lord Vetinari believes in a common or natural law; if a man can sell short-weight bread and get away with it he does. If, however, his defrauded customers decide to nail him to his own ceiling, then that is fine, too.

Criminal Justice

Although the current system iin Ankh-Morpork consists almost entirely of 'Guild Justice' enforced by the city's Guilds, it is still the case that criminals taken by the Watch may opt to stand trial before the Patrician.

The Historical System

In the very early days of Ankh-Morpork justice was dispensed by the Archchancellor's Court. There then followed a system set up under the city's monarchy, and many of today's traditions date from that period.

Probate Court

Small cases, involving the common citizens, are heard by the Court of Magisters made up of the city's ruling classes. Larger cases, involving the wealthier members of society, are pleaded on their behalf by the Guild of Lawyers.

Tip of the Day

The death penalty is usually reserved for treachery to the city, continuing to commit murder after being told not to, irredeemable stupidity while not being a troll, and persistent street theatre.
